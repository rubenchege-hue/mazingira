Data Quality Assessment Tier 1–3
The GHG Protocol defines three tiers of data quality. Tier 1 = default factors. Tier 2 = country/region-specific. Tier 3 = site-specific / direct measurement. The goal for assurance is Tier 2+ for material sources.
TierSource TypeExamplesStatus
Tier 1Default factorsIPCC defaults, DEFRA averages○
Tier 2Country-specificKenya grid factor, local fuel specs○
Tier 3Site-specificStack monitoring, CEMS, mass balance○
Tier determined by the highest tier achieved for your most material emission source. Verifiers typically expect Tier 2+ for >80% of total emissions.

Inventory Management Plan IMP documentation
A GHG Inventory Management Plan (IMP) is required for third-party verification. Use this template structure to document your inventory process. Download the checklist to use as a gap analysis tool.
IMP Requirements
  • Organisational boundary description
  • Operational scope (S1, S2, S3 definitions)
  • Base year and recalculation policy
  • Emission factor sources and GWP values
  • Data collection procedures
  • Quality assurance/quality control (QA/QC)
  • Uncertainty assessment methodology
  • Documentation and record retention
  • Management review and approval
Verification Requirements (ISO 14064-3)
  • Verification scope and boundary agreed
  • Materiality threshold documented
  • Site visits and data sampling plan
  • Third-party verifier qualifications
  • Limited or reasonable assurance level
  • Emissions data recalculated independently
  • Evidence traceable to source documents
  • Verification opinion / assurance statement
