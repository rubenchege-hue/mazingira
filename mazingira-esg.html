EU regulations — key frameworks
EU MRL Regulation (EC) 396/2005
Sets maximum pesticide residue levels for all food and feed imported into the EU. Updated regularly. EU retailers often impose stricter private standards (e.g. 50–70% of legal MRL). Non-compliance = border rejection.
EU Plant Health Reg. (EU) 2019/2072
Phytosanitary certification required via KEPHIS. Lists quarantine pests (False Codling Moth, Thrips palmi). EU increased inspection frequency on Kenyan roses in 2023 — strict compliance needed.
CSDDD — Corporate Sustainability Due Diligence
EU Corporate Sustainability Due Diligence Directive (2024) requires large EU buyers to vet supply chains for human rights & environmental harms. Kenyan exporters must provide labour, water, and land-use documentation.
EU Organic Regulation (EU) 2018/848
Strict certification for organic herbs/flowers sold in EU. Requires third-party verification (e.g. Ceres, Ecocert, EnCert). No use of synthetic pesticides; documented traceability from farm to EU border.
EU-Kenya Economic Partnership Agreement (EPA)
Duty-free, quota-free access for Kenyan exports. Includes enforceable sustainability provisions: Paris Agreement, ILO labour standards, biodiversity commitments. Non-compliance risks preferential access.
